Welcome below

THE MINES REMEMBER

Beneath five shifting suns, rival crews mine gemstones, trade with dangerous guilds, and build reputations that may not survive the night. Prestige wins the expedition, but every shortcut leaves evidence.

Objective

Finish the fourth Moon with the most Prestige. Build points during play, then add retired cards, Bandit guild sets, Thief legacies, Tavern missions, gold, and complete gem sets. Finally subtract Reputation penalties.

Game profile

Players: 2–4
Structure: 16 days, grouped into 4 Moons
Core systems: card-driven worker placement, engine building, set collection, theft, sabotage, debt, and hidden information.

The table contract

Golden rules

  1. Card text modifies this rulebook. If a card gives a specific exception, follow the card.
  2. Resolve actions from left to right. You cannot jump past an unused earlier action.
  3. Linked actions resolve together. Spend one legal die for every linked space.
  4. “Any gem” is chosen by the player who pays or gains it.
  5. Traps are not gems. They never satisfy gem costs, sets, or missions.

Timing rule

Complete the current action and all of its mandatory effects before resolving an additional action, triggered effect, or ending the turn. When several effects wait at the same timing window, resolve the active player’s effects in card order, then continue in player order.

Rules Truth: Planning builds card pools. Day spends dice from those pools. Night uses one card per turn. Moon penalizes unpaid debt before presence. Final scoring audits every source.

01 · Before play

SETUP

  1. Place the Main Board. Arrange the five sectors from Sunrise through Sunset.
  2. Choose player colors and names. Give each player 5 Miner dice, their Miner markers, and their 10-card Starting deck.
  3. Take starting resources: 10⭐, 5💰, 1 gem of each color, 0 Debt, 0 Traps, and Reputation position 1.
  4. Prepare Bank tiles. Shuffle Bronze, Silver, and Gold pools separately. Reveal 3 tiles from each tier.
  5. Prepare the card displays. Shuffle and reveal 5 Market cards and 3 Thief cards. Place all five 7-card Bandit guild stacks and the Crownless King where everyone can see them. Shuffle the Tavern deck.
  6. Prepare the Tavern bag. Add 5 additional Miner dice of every participating player color and 10 Neutral Miner dice. Reveal 5 dice as the Tavern offer.
  7. Choose the First Player. Player 1 begins the first Planning phase. Place all Prestige and Reputation markers.

Starting deck

Each player has one Base card for each Day shift (RP01–RP05) and five advanced Starting cards (RP06–RP10). Shuffle all 10 together.

Base cards carry a ⚙ mark. They are permanent: they cannot be sold, retired, stolen, or lost. Whenever they leave play, return them to the bottom of their owner’s deck.

Public and private information

Prestige, played cards, board markers, and public displays are open information. A player’s hand, unplayed deck, gems, gold, traps, Debt, Reputation, and owned-card area are private in Shared Network play. Shared Screen play uses table trust and pass-the-screen etiquette.

First game: Ignore advanced tactical planning. Learn the rhythm: assign cards, spend matching dice, use one Night card per turn, then pay Debt before Moon scoring.

Mine row

Mine spaces hold Miner or Bandit markers. The earliest open spaces may show board bonuses. Existing presence, Tools, Bandits, and the printed card action can increase production.

Operation row

Operation spaces record presence and allow the operation only when the action space on the played card actually names that operation. A reward-only action placed in an Operation row grants only its printed reward.

Presence

Every Mine and Operation is a separate presence contest at Moon scoring. Common Miners, Bandit markers, and “presence-only” markers count. Presence-only markers never trigger placement or operation effects.

Capacity

An action is legal only if the board has enough empty spaces for every required marker. A linked 3-space action needs 3 empty spaces and 3 legal dice before it highlights.

Sabotaged section: A section means both rows of one Day transition. For example, the Noon section includes the Noon Mine and the Noon Market row.

Turn order

Resolve players in the current player order. After each Moon, reorder from least Prestige to most Prestige. Ties reverse their previous relative order. The first player in the new order receives the First Player marker.

02 · Reading the table

CARD ANATOMY

Day side

  1. ID and shift color identify the card and its natural pool.
  2. Mining row shows one or more action spaces and their gem output.
  3. Operation row shows Operations and/or direct Gold or Prestige benefits.
  4. Chains connect actions that must be paid and resolved together.
  5. Reputation row changes costs or rewards according to Safe, Watched, or Suspicious status.
  6. ⊕ Additional action is optional after the main Operation resolves.

Night side

  1. Steal states how many gems are attempted.
  2. Sabotage removes a common Miner from the board.
  3. Guard protects its owner until triggered or Night ends.
  4. Suspicion shows evidence gained on use, traps, or guards.
  5. Retire value scores only if the card is retired. ⚙ Base cards return instead.

Rogue Miners lasts four Moons. Each Moon contains four days. Each day follows the same rhythm.

One day at a glance

Planning

Each player draws up to a five-card planning capacity, rolls all available Miner dice, auto-assigns ready Bandit dice, and assigns cards to matching shift pools.

Day

Resolve Sunrise, Morning, Noon, Afternoon, and Sunset. Players spend dice only from the current shift’s card pool.

Night

Unspent common dice and ready Thief dice power Night actions. A player may activate only one card per Night turn.

Preparation

Resolve guards, return unused special dice, refill displays, advance the day, and begin Planning again.

End of a Moon

  1. Players may repay Debt.
  2. Every unpaid Debt costs 1⭐.
  3. Score presence in all 10 board sections.
  4. Refresh displays, special dice, and board state.
  5. Set the new player order.
End of game: After presence scoring for the fourth Moon, preserve the final board state for Tavern missions, then proceed directly to Final Scoring.

Ending a turn

Players explicitly press END or PASS. The game does not automatically pass merely because one action was taken. This matters for cards with several independent spaces. Undo is available until the player ends their turn.

04 · Phase rules

PLANNING

On your Planning turn

  1. Draw & Roll. Draw until your hand plus any carried assigned cards equals 5. Roll every available Miner die independently to Sunrise, Morning, Noon, Afternoon, Sunset, or Night.
  2. Auto-assign Bandits. Each ready Bandit die enters its matching Day pool if a legal card can support it. The Crownless King’s owner chooses its Day pool.
  3. Assign cards. Place at most one card in each shift slot. A card can be assigned only where at least one matching common or Bandit die exists.
  4. Use optional Planning actions. Change shifts, take Debt, repay Debt when permitted, or make the once-per-turn gem-to-gold trade.
  5. End Planning. Unassigned common Day dice move to Night. Unassigned Bandit dice return to their owner’s Bandit area.

Shift Change

Pay 1💰 to move a selected common die from one shift pool to another legal shift. The action is enabled whenever the player has at least 1💰.

Free trade

Once per turn, choose any 3 real gems—same or different—and gain 1💰. The button disables after use and resets on the player’s next turn.

Important Planning rules

  • A card remains assigned even if all dice are moved from its pool to Night.
  • A Bandit cannot auto-assign to a card that is already on its Night side.
  • Bandit and common dice can share a pool, but every die still needs a printed action space.
  • Base cards always remain in the player’s possession. They cannot enter sale or retirement choices.
  • Undo returns assigned cards to the hand and restores free actions, resources, dice, and logs to their prior state.
Example: Mira rolls two Morning dice, one Sunset die, and two Night dice. She may assign one Morning card and one Sunset card. Both Morning dice share that card’s pool; they do not create extra actions beyond the spaces printed on the card.
PLANNING
05 · Phase rules

DAY PHASE

Resolve the five Day shifts in order. Only dice in the current shift pool may activate that card’s Day actions.

Taking a Day turn

  1. Select a legal common or Bandit die from the current pool.
  2. Choose the next legal action space or linked group on that card.
  3. Confirm enough matching dice and board spaces exist.
  4. Place one marker per action space, spend one die per action space, and resolve printed effects left to right.
  5. Resolve the Operation’s mandatory choice, then its optional additional action.
  6. Continue with other available actions or press END/PASS.

Unlinked actions

Each space is independent. Using one does not consume later spaces, but later spaces stay locked until earlier spaces are resolved in order.

Linked actions

All connected spaces resolve together. Place every marker and spend every required die. A Bandit die used in a linked group still requires the remaining common dice.

Action versus benefit

A printed Operation name performs that Operation. A printed Gold, Prestige, or gem benefit grants only that benefit, even though its marker is placed in the Operation row.

Board capacity

Never highlight or choose an action if the destination row lacks enough empty spaces for the full action group.

Mining

Gain the gems printed on the card, plus applicable board-space, presence, Tool, and Bandit bonuses. If an action grants Any gems, choose each color. Linked mining actions combine all printed production and bonuses before the action is logged.

Example: An Azure Syndicate Bandit and a common Miner activate two linked blue mining spaces showing 3 and 4 Blue. The guild adds +1 Blue and one existing friendly marker adds the applicable presence bonus. Resolve the entire linked group once, then record the complete gain.

Finishing a shift

When a player has no legal actions left, PASS blinks. Any unused common dice from that Day pool move to Night. Unused Bandit dice return to the player’s Bandit area. Continue until every player is finished, then advance to the next Day shift.

DAY PHASE
06 · Five operations

OPERATIONS

Bank

Buy one affordable Contract or Tool. Contracts cost their printed gems and immediately grant Gold and Prestige. Tools cost printed Gold and grant immediate Prestige plus a permanent modifier.

Bank reputation modifiers apply to the card’s main action. Tool prices are not modified by Reputation. A shuffle Tool gives one optional shuffle per Bank visit; buying remains available whether or not it is used.

Bandit Guilds

Buy one Bandit. A normal guild member costs 5 gems of its guild color, plus 1 matching gem for every Bandit of that guild already owned, then apply the played card, Reputation, Tool, and Bandit modifiers.

The Crownless King costs 8 Any gems and requires at least 3 other Bandits.

Market

Buy one of 5 face-up Market cards. Pay its effective Gold cost after the assigned card’s Reputation row, Tools, linked-action benefits, and Bandit modifiers. Put the purchased card on the bottom of your deck.

A shuffle Tool gives one optional shuffle per Market visit.

Thieves Den

Buy one of 3 face-up Thief cards. Pay its effective Gold and Any-gem cost, choosing every gem spent. Gain the card’s instant Prestige and its numbered Thief die immediately.

Reputation, Tools, and Azure Syndicate can discount Gold, gems, or a chosen combination as stated.

Tavern

Draw the number of Tavern missions allowed by the card, Reputation, Tools, and Bandits; choose 1 and return the rest. Tavern missions score at game end.

The additional action may buy your colored Miner dice or Neutral dice from the 5-die offer at the card’s effective price.

Additional actions

After completing a main Operation, you may use the ⊕ effect printed on that card. Starting cards provide basic versions; Market cards provide stronger, card-specific versions. Additional actions never replace the mandatory main action.

Typical effects include Bank exchanges, reducing Suspicion, selling a card, buying Traps, and buying Tavern dice.

OPERATIONS
07 · Risk economy

DEBT & REPUTATION

Debt

Taking Debt

Whenever Gold is needed and the rules permit it, take 1 Debt to gain 1💰 and immediately lose 1⭐. Repeat as necessary.

Repaying Debt

Before Moon scoring, pay 2💰 to remove 1 Debt and recover 1⭐. You may repay as many as you can afford.

Unpaid Debt

After repayment, lose 1⭐ for every Debt still held. This happens at every Moon, including Moon 4. Debt is therefore cumulative pressure, not only an end-game penalty.

No double penalty: Debt is not subtracted again during Final Scoring because Moon 4 already applied it.

Maximum suspicion

When a player reaches 15, they are exposed: pay 3💰, taking Debt for any missing Gold, then move the Reputation marker to 6 (Watched).

Final Reputation penalty

Lose Prestige equal to Reputation minus 1. Position 1 loses 0⭐; position 10 loses 9⭐; position 15 loses 14⭐.

08 · Special workers

BANDITS

Buying and owning

  • Buying a Bandit adds its card and matching Bandit marker to your permanent collection.
  • Normal cost: 5 guild-colored gems +1 matching gem per Bandit of that guild already owned, then modifiers.
  • Bandit markers use the guild’s gem color and the owner’s player-color background.
  • A Bandit may be used once per Moon. Used Bandits refresh for the next Moon.

Planning and placement

On Draw & Roll, each ready Bandit auto-enters its corresponding Day pool when a suitable card is assigned. Select the Bandit before choosing the board action. A Bandit in the card area cannot be placed directly onto the board.

A Bandit can activate its matching Mine or its matching Operation. On a linked group, the Bandit pays for one space; every remaining linked space requires another legal die. If action spaces remain after placement, the player may continue using dice before ending the turn.

Active and passive timing

At Start or End of Day, a placed Bandit uses its Active line; an unplaced ready Bandit uses its Passive line. Resolve confirmation reports in player order. Keep effects protect chosen gems through Night.

The Crownless King

Requirement: own at least 3 other Bandits. Cost: 8 Any gems after modifiers. Choose its Day pool each Planning phase. Its Mine bonus is +1 gem per owned Bandit; its Operation bonus is +1⭐ per owned Bandit; its Active End-of-Day ability gains +1⭐ per owned Bandit.

It may be bought from another player’s unassigned Bandit area; payment goes to the Bank. It remains gray regardless of owner. It scores a fixed 15⭐ at game end and does not join another guild set.

09 · Special workers

THIEVES

Buying and readiness

  • Pay the printed Gold and Any-gem cost after modifiers. Choose every Any gem spent.
  • Gain the printed instant Prestige, then add the card and numbered Thief die to your area.
  • At Start of Night, every ready Thief die moves to its owner’s Night pool.
  • To use one, select that Thief die before selecting a legal Guard, Steal, or Sabotage action.
  • After use, return it disabled to its owner’s area. Each Thief die is usable once per Moon.
  • Unused Thief dice return ready after Night. All disabled Thieves refresh at the Moon boundary.

Three layers of a Thief card

Passive

Works only while that Thief die is ready and unused. Triggered, Start-of-Night, and End-of-Night effects resolve at their named timing.

Action bonus

Added only when that numbered die performs its preferred Steal, Sabotage, or Guard action.

Legacy

When using the Thief, count all owned cards of that den, enabled or disabled. Apply the matching 2–3, 4–5, or 6-card tier.

Legacy end-game Prestige

Every Thief card scores according to the number of cards of its own den you possess:

No normal suspicion: A Steal or Sabotage performed with any Thief die ignores normal suspicion from using the action, sabotaging the same target, and being blocked by a normal Trap or Guard. Separate card effects may still add Suspicion.

Den identities

Shadow avoids defense and steals gems. Rogue attacks Gold and Prestige. Trickster manipulates board Miners after Sabotage. Guardian defends and counterattacks. Smuggler converts stolen gems. Phantom removes presence, Prestige, and Suspicion.

10 · Phase rules

NIGHT PHASE

Night turn structure

  1. Choose one assigned card. You may use actions from that card only this turn.
  2. Select a common Night die, or first select a ready Thief die.
  3. Resolve the next legal Night action or linked group from left to right.
  4. Complete all target choices, results, Thief bonuses, and Sabotage bonuses.
  5. You may continue with remaining actions on that same card if legal.
  6. Press END/PASS. You cannot return to that card on a later Night turn.

Steal

Choose a valid target and attempt to take the printed number of random gems from their bag unless an effect says “open.” Kept gems are excluded.

Guard

Leave the die on the card. It blocks the next robbery unless ignored. A Guard remains until triggered or Night ends.

Sabotage

Choose a target, then remove one of that player’s common Miner markers. Bandit markers cannot be sabotaged.

Card resolution

After all usable actions on an unguarded card finish, choose:

  • Return: put it on the bottom of your deck.
  • Retire: place it in your Retires for its printed end-game value.

Base cards cannot retire and automatically return. A card holding a Guard does not resolve immediately; wait until the Guard is triggered or Night ends, then choose Return or Retire.

End of Night

  1. Resolve all remaining Guards and their card exits.
  2. Resolve End-of-Night Thief passives, Trickster trades before Phantom effects.
  3. Return kept gems to their owners.
  4. Return unused Thief dice ready; used Thief dice remain disabled.
  5. Return unused common Night dice according to their origin and prepare the next day.
NIGHT PHASE
10 · Conflict resolution

ROBBERY & SABOTAGE

Robbery sequence

  1. Declare target and calculate the attempted gem count.
  2. Apply normal suspicion unless a Thief die cancels it.
  3. Check Guard. If blocked, steal no gems, apply Guard effects and blocking suspicion, then resolve that Guard card when required.
  4. Draw from the target’s bag. Real gems and Traps are mixed; kept gems are not available.
  5. If any Trap is drawn: discard every drawn Trap, return all drawn real gems to the target, and apply the blocking penalty. The robbery gains no gems.
  6. If successful: transfer the gems, then resolve Thief action and Legacy bonuses.
Multiple draws, one result: If a robbery draws several gems and at least one is a Trap, the entire stolen group is returned. Traps are discarded.

Sabotage sequence

  1. Declare a target who has at least one common Miner on the board.
  2. If required, apply suspicion for sabotaging the player robbed earlier.
  3. Select and remove one target Miner. This is the standard Sabotage.
  4. If a used Thief has a Sabotage Bonus and the current action allows Sabotage, show its instruction, then interact with highlighted legal board choices.
  5. Resolve every additional move, swap, placement, removal, Prestige, or Suspicion effect; then end the bonus.

Presence-only Miners

Markers added by T15, T18, or T35 count only for Moon presence. They do not activate the board space, grant a placement bonus, perform an Operation, or trigger worker abilities.

Example: T13 sabotages a Miner in the Noon Market row. After the standard removal, T13 may move one of the active player’s Miners from either the Noon Mine or Noon Market to any empty space in either Noon row.

11 · Every fourth day

MOON SCORING

Resolve in this exact order

  1. Debt payment window. Each player may pay 2💰 per Debt to remove it and recover 1⭐.
  2. Unpaid Debt penalty. Lose 1⭐ for each Debt still held.
  3. Presence scoring. Score every Mine and every Operation separately.
  4. Snapshot. On Moon 4, preserve the board for end-game missions before clearing markers.
  5. Refresh. Clear board markers, refresh displays, return protected resources, and ready special dice for the next Moon.
  6. New order. Sort players from least Prestige to most Prestige.

Count common Miners, Bandits, and presence-only Miners. A Mine and its corresponding Operation are separate contests.

Example: In Sunrise Mine, Ada has 3 markers, Bor has 2, and Cira has 1. Ada gains 3⭐. In Sunrise Bank, Ada and Bor each have 2; both gain 1⭐.

What refreshes?

  • Disabled Bandit and Thief dice become available for the next Moon.
  • Bank, Market, Thief, and Tavern displays refresh according to their supply rules.
  • Player order changes; prior ties reverse relative order.
  • Debt remains until paid and will hurt again next Moon.

Tavern resolution order

Resolve normal Tavern missions in owned-card order, spending required Gold and gems. Then resolve zero-resource and comparison missions in this order: TVN18, TVN25, TVN24, TVN33. Finally score remaining Gold and complete gem sets. A resource exchanged for a Tavern mission cannot score again.

Gem set example: After exchanges, a player has Yellow 9, Green 6, Red 8, Blue 4, Violet 7. The smallest pile is Blue 4, so the player has 4 complete sets and gains 4⭐.

Winner and ties

The highest total Prestige wins. The digital prototype breaks equal totals by more Gold in the final supply record, then less Debt, then player-seat order.

Audit tools

Final INFO lists every scoring source. Prestige History shows when points changed. Game Statistics records peaks and play time. Game Analysis compares action use, economy flow, and scoring drivers.

13 · Digital prototype

PLAY MODES & CONTROLS

Shared Screen

Two to four players use one computer. Enter custom names and optionally mark any seat as a Bot. Pass control to the active player and avoid looking at private hands or resources.

Shared Network

Players use separate computers on the same Wi-Fi/LAN. The Host creates a lobby; other players join with the shown address and 5-character room code. The Host starts when 2–4 seats are connected.

Network privacy and authority

  • Only the active player’s device can send game actions. The Host validates and resolves them.
  • Each player sees their own hand, deck information, resources, Reputation, Debt, traps, and owned cards.
  • Opponents see only public Prestige in their statistics area.
  • Cards become public when assigned to Day or Night pools.
  • Phase transitions and choice ownership synchronize to all devices.

Undo

Undo restores the complete game state before the latest action: resources, dice, markers, cards, statistics, pending choices, and logs. It is available during Planning, Day, and Night until the active player presses END/PASS. Undo never reopens a resolved gem-choice popup as a new action.

Bot priorities

Bots follow deterministic test priorities: operations before mines, linked actions first, Bandits before common dice, same-guild and same-den purchases, highest-Prestige Night targets, debt payment when affordable, and lowest-held gem color for Any choices. Bots retire eligible cards rather than return them.

Portable application

Launch RogueMiners.exe or RogueMiners-Portable.exe. The application starts a local server and opens fullscreen. The Host should allow Windows Firewall access on Private networks. Use EXIT to close the application and server cleanly.

Connection rule: Joiners must use the Host’s LAN address—not 127.0.0.1—and the exact room code shown in the Host lobby.

BANK TOOL REFERENCE

Tools are permanent. Their purchase price is not changed by Reputation. Tool Prestige is gained immediately.
